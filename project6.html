Goal

This analysis aimed to gain a deeper understanding of several crucial aspects related to October 2020 within this call center. I sought to uncover essential insights such as incoming contacts' volume, origins, and distribution over time. Additionally, I targeted factors such as the average response time of the assistants, identified the primary factors triggering these contacts, and assessed how clients evaluate the call center's service quality.

Main Questions

  1. What was the total volume of contacts received?
  2. What were the origins of these contacts?
  3. Did we meet the Service Level Agreement (SLA) for handling customer contacts?
  4. How much time, on average, did the assistant take on each contact?
  5. What were the primary reasons that prompted individuals to make these contacts?
  6. How do customers evaluate the quality of service provided by the call center?

Conclusions

  1. In the month of October 2020, our call center handled a total of 32,941 customer interactions.
  2. On a daily basis, our call center receives an average of 1,050 to 1,150 customer contacts, with the majority originating from the call center itself, accounting for 10,639 interactions. Additionally, our chatbot handles 8,256 contacts, demonstrating its significant role in customer engagement. These interactions span across the entire United States, with notable concentrations in California (3,631 contacts), Texas (3,572 contacts), and Florida (2,834 contacts).
  3. The majority of interactions, totaling 28,773, are efficiently handled within (20625 contacts) or below (8148 contacts) the Service Level Agreement (SLA). However, it's worth noting that 4,168 individuals had waiting times above the SLA.
  4. The average interaction duration is 25 minutes.
  5. The primary reason for the majority of customer contacts is billing inquiries, accounting for 23,462 interactions. Additionally, we received contacts related to payments (4,749 interactions) and service outages (4,730 interactions).
  6. The average CSAT score of 5.55 suggests a fair, albeit not exceptional, level of customer satisfaction. It's important to note that a significant portion of our customers (20,670) did not provide a score, and this should be taken into consideration when evaluating our overall satisfaction levels.
