Goal

The client has two properties in Seattle and wants to determine the optimal listing strategy for each property on Airbnb. This project aims to analyse the distribution of Airbnb listings in Seattle, including pricing and property characteristics, to provide insights on when and which property to list on Airbnb.

Client properties details

Emily worked hard and stayed committed to her goal of owning two beautiful homes in Seattle. Her efforts paid off, and she finally achieved her goal. She owned one property in the lovely Queen Anne neighborhood, which had six bedrooms and four bathrooms, and another in Delridge, which had four bedrooms and 3.5 bathrooms. As Emily's circumstances changed, she decided to put one of her homes on AirBnB and focus on enjoying the other. However, Emily faced a challenging decision: which property should she list? Both homes had unique features and advantages, making it difficult for her to determine which one would be more beneficial.

Disclaimer: The client and any properties associated with them mentioned or described in this project are entirely fictional and have been created solely to complete this project. However, all the information analyzed in this project regarding Airbnb listings in Seattle during the year 2016 is factual.

Main Questions

  1. What is the distribution of listings in Seattle as well as the average price per listing for each corresponding zip code?
  2. What is the correlation between the number of bedrooms and the price of the listing?
  3. Are there similarities between the listings producing high revenue and Emily’s properties?
  4. What is the pattern of revenue changes throughout the year in Seattle, and during which season is it optimal to list a property on Airbnb in Seattle?

Conclusions

  1. In terms of Airbnb listings, Delridge has a smaller number of options available, and the average price per listing is $82.1. In contrast, Queen Anne has a higher concentration of listings, but the average price per listing is $166.4.
  2. Properties that offer 6 bedrooms are listed at an average price of $578.2, which is significantly higher than the average listing price of $313.7 for places with 4 bedrooms.
  3. Given that Emily's property shares many similarities with the listing named "New Modern House - Amazing Water View," such as a close location, modern design, and comparable size (except Emily's additional room), it is reasonable to compare the two properties and to assume that Emily’s property has real potential on Airbnb.
  4. Our revenue data shows two distinct peaks - one during the summer and the other in December. It is reasonable to assume that these peaks are caused by families going on vacation during the summer months or visiting family members during the Christmas holidays.
    5. While Emily loves both properties, based on the data presented, it appears reasonable to conclude that listing the Queen Anne property is a wise decision that will allow her to focus on enjoying her other home while securing a good return on her investment.
