Goal

The goal of this project is to assess the impact of COVID-19 globally, with a specific focus on Portugal, and to investigate the role of vaccination in reducing COVID-19 deaths. To achieve this goal, the project will analyze data on COVID-19 cases and deaths worldwide, and in Portugal specifically, over time. The project will also analyze data on vaccination rates in Portugal and investigate the relationship between vaccination and COVID-19 deaths.

Main Questions

  1. What’s the global impact of COVID-19, including the number of confirmed cases and fatalities?
  2. Did Portugal implement effective measures to control the Covid-19 pandemic, or did it experience a high number of cases and deaths?
  3. Has Portugal administered COVID-19 vaccines to a higher percentage of its population than the global average? Additionally, what is the total number of vaccines distributed in Portugal and how many individuals have been vaccinated thus far?
  4. What has been the impact of the COVID-19 vaccination campaign on reducing COVID cases and deaths in Portugal?

Conclusions

  1. The global impact of COVID-19 has been substantial, with 669,111,131 confirmed cases and 6,842,436 fatalities reported worldwide. This pandemic has significantly affected various aspects of life, including healthcare systems, economies, education, and social interactions. One of the ways we can visualize the global impact of COVID-19 is through the map charts in the report. These demonstrate that countries with the highest number of confirmed cases are not necessarily the ones with the highest number of deaths. The variations may be due to several factors, such as differences in healthcare systems, population demographics, and government responses.
  2. Portugal has been significantly impacted by COVID-19, with 54,207 confirmed cases reported for every 100,000 inhabitants. However, the fatality rate per 100,000 cases is comparatively low, with only 469 deaths reported. While the number of cases reported in Portugal is concerning, it is essential to note that the fatality rate may be influenced by several factors, such as the country's healthcare system, age demographics, and government response. Nonetheless, the Portuguese government has implemented several measures to mitigate the impact of COVID-19, including vaccination campaigns, social distancing protocols, and the mandatory use of masks in public spaces. We can confirm that there were two peaks in COVID-19 cases: one after the holiday season of 2020, as families gathered together, and another in 2022, when measures to prevent the spread of the virus were reduced. It's likely that the number of cases began to increase at a slower rate because testing has decreased since the summer of 2022.
  3. Portugal has surpassed the global average in terms of COVID-19 vaccine administration, with an impressive rate of 95,228 out of every 100,000 people vaccinated, compared to the global average of only 10,478 out of every 100,000 people. It is worth noting that global values are calculated based only on countries for which there is available data. Moreover, the total number of COVID-19 vaccines administered in Portugal is 28,106,464, covering 9,779,388 individuals. This suggests that, on average, each person has received about three vaccine doses.
  4. The data presented in the report charts suggests that as the number of vaccinated individuals increases, the curve on the death charts slows down. This observation may indicate that the vaccination campaign in Portugal has been successful in reducing the impact of COVID-19 on the population. However, it is important to note that there may be other factors at play, such as changes in government policies or improvements in healthcare systems.
    5. It is important to note that the numbers provided have changed since the knowledge cutoff date, as the pandemic continues to evolve and affect people globally.

Data cleaning:
    MS Excel
  • Remove fields that were not considered in this study, such as gender, age, testing, etc.
  • Change the date format to YYYY-MM-DD to be compatible with SQL.
  • Divide the single table into two distinct tables: one for vaccination data and the other for cases and deaths data.
  • Replace null values in the fields “total_cases,” “new_cases,” “total_deaths,” and “new_deaths” with 0.

    • MS SQL Server
  • Remove all rows with null values for the following fields in the vaccination table: “total_vaccinations,” “people_vaccinated,” “people_fully_vaccinated,” “total_boosters,” and “new_vaccinations.” Since our database includes dates from the beginning of the pandemic when vaccines were not yet available, many of these fields were not applicable or did not have data. Removing these rows will ensure that the data we analyse is accurate and relevant to our study.
  • Remove all rows with null values in the “continent” field for both the vaccination table and the cases and deaths table. This is necessary because some location values like "Upper middle income" and "Africa" are not specific enough to be classified as a location and all odd location values have null values in the “continent field”.
  • Remove all rows with null values in the following fields from the cases_and_deaths table: “total_cases,” “new_cases,” “total_deaths,” and “new_deaths”. By removing rows with null values, we will have a dataset with only significant values, allowing us to draw more accurate conclusions from our analysis.
SQL queries: 

USE covid19;

-- List of countries --
SELECT 
	Distinct(location)
FROM dbo.cases_and_deaths;

-- Total cases and deaths by location --

SELECT
	location,
	total_cases,
	total_deaths
FROM dbo.cases_and_deaths
WHERE date IN
	(SELECT MAX(date)
	FROM dbo.cases_and_deaths)
ORDER BY location ASC;

-- Portugal cases and deaths through the time --

SELECT 
	date,
	total_cases,
	total_deaths
FROM dbo.cases_and_deaths
WHERE location = 'Portugal'
ORDER BY date ASC;

-- Total global (excluding Portugal) cases through the time --

SELECT 
	date,
	SUM(total_cases) as cases_global,
	SUM(total_deaths) as deaths_global
FROM dbo.cases_and_deaths
WHERE location <> 'Portugal'
GROUP by date
ORDER BY date ASC;

-- People vaccinated in Portugal through the time  --	
SELECT
	date,
	people_vaccinated
FROM dbo.vaccination
WHERE location = 'Portugal'
ORDER BY date ASC;

-- People vaccinated globally (excluding Portugal) through the time --

SELECT 
	date,
	SUM(people_vaccinated) as total_global
FROM dbo.vaccination
WHERE location <> 'Portugal'
GROUP by date
ORDER BY date ASC;

-- Total of vaccines distributed in Portugal --

SELECT 
	date,
	total_vaccinations 
FROM dbo.vaccination
WHERE 
	location = 'Portugal' AND
	date IN 
		(SELECT 
			MAX(date)
		FROM dbo.vaccination
		WHERE location = 'Portugal');

-- Total of people vaccinated in Portugal --

SELECT 
	date,
	people_vaccinated
FROM dbo.vaccination
WHERE 
	location = 'Portugal' AND
	date IN 
		(SELECT 
			MAX(date)
		FROM dbo.vaccination
		WHERE location = 'Portugal');

-- People vaccinated globally by location excluding Portugal --
SELECT
	location,
	MAX(people_vaccinated)
FROM dbo.vaccination 
WHERE location <> 'Portugal'
GROUP BY location
ORDER BY location;
